Idukki- The spice garden of Kerala

Idukki, a district in the southwestern Indian state of Kerala, is renowned for its mountainous terrain, vast forests, and spice plantations. It's known as the "Land of Spices" and the "Queen of the Western Ghats," boasting the largest arch dam in Asia, the Idukki Dam. Geographically, Idukki is characterized by its hilly landscape and dense forests, with over half the district covered by natural reserves according to idukki.nic.in. Beyond its natural beauty, Idukki is also an important contributor to Kerala's power supply, with a significant portion of the state's electricity generated by its hydroelectric projects.
