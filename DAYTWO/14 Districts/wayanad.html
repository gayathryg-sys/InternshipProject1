Wayanad-"Land of Paddy Fields"

Wayanad, a district in the northeast of Kerala, India, is a picturesque plateau nestled in the Western Ghats. Known for its lush green landscapes and spice plantations, it's often referred to as the "green paradise". The name "Wayanad" is believed to be derived from the Malayalam words "vayal" and "naad," meaning "land of paddy fields," reflecting the region's agricultural heritage. Wayanad is not only a haven for nature lovers with its scenic beauty, but also a region rich in history and culture, with a significant tribal population
