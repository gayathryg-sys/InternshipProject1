Kozhikode-"City of Spices"

Kozhikode, also known as Calicut, is a vibrant city on the Malabar Coast of Kerala, India, renowned for its rich history and cultural significance. It was once a major trading hub for spices and served as the capital of the powerful Zamorin kingdom. Today, Kozhikode is celebrated for its beaches, historical sites, and unique cultural traditions, including Mappilappattu, Kolkali, and Oppana. The city's lanes, bazaars, and business hubs still resonate with a sense of history, particularly the arrival of Vasco da Gama in 1498 according to Kerala Tourism.
