Alappuzha-"Venice of the East"

Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is a city in the southern Indian state of Kerala, renowned for its backwaters, beaches, and coir industry. Often called the "Venice of the East", Alappuzha's network of canals, lagoons, and rivers, alongside the Arabian Sea, offers stunning scenery and unique houseboat experiences. The town's history as a major port, coupled with its serene natural beauty, makes it a popular tourist destination.

BACKWATERS AND HOUSEBOAT CRUISES

Alappuzha is famous for its serene backwaters and luxurious houseboat cruises, offering a unique and tranquil experience amidst the beauty of Kerala's waterways.

AMBALAPUZHA TEMPLE

The Ambalapuzha Sree Krishna Temple, located in Alappuzha, Kerala, is a renowned Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, known for its Kerala-style architecture and the famous "Palpayasam" offering.

CHAMPAKULAM CHURCH

Champakulam Church, also known as St. Mary's Forane Church, is one of the oldest churches in Kerala, believed to be established in the 5th century and located in Champakulam village.
